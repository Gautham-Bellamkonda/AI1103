Let $S$ be a sample space and two mutually exclusive events $A$ and $B$ be such that $A + B = S$. If $P(\cdot)$ denotes the probability of the event, the maximum value of $P(A)P(B)$ is
\section{Solution}
Given that $A$ and $B$ are mutually exclusive events.
\begin{align}
\Pr(A+B) &= 1\\
\Pr(A) + \Pr(B) &= 1 \\
\Pr(A)\Pr(B) &= \Pr(A) - (\Pr(A))^2\\
&= \dfrac{1}{4} - \left( \Pr(A) - \dfrac{1}{2} \right)^2 \\
&\leq \dfrac{1}{4}\\
\Pr(A) = \Pr(B) = \dfrac{1}{2}, &\Rightarrow \Pr(A)\Pr(B) = \dfrac{1}{4}\\ \therefore \text{max}(\Pr(A)\Pr(B)) &= \dfrac{1}{4}.
\end{align}
